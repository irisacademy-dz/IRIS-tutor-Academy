Course Modules

The IRIS Tutor Training MOOC consists of four comprehensive modules, each focusing on key aspects of online English language teaching. Complete all modules and pass the final assessment to earn your certification.

Module 1: Foundations of Applied Language Teaching for Adults

Introduction: This module introduces fundamental principles of adult learning and key theories that underpin effective language instruction. You'll explore how adults acquire language differently and the importance of learner-centered approaches.

  • Andragogy vs. Pedagogy: Understanding adult learning principles
  • Key Learning Theories: Constructivism, Sociocultural Theory
  • Motivation in Adult Learners: Intrinsic and extrinsic factors
  • Communicative Language Teaching (CLT): Core tenets and application
  • Learner Needs Analysis: Identifying goals and preferences

Module 2: Pedagogical Tools and Techniques in Online Language Teaching

Introduction: This module focuses on practical methodologies and digital tools essential for engaging online language lessons. You'll learn how to leverage technology to create dynamic and interactive learning experiences.

  • Synchronous vs. Asynchronous Learning: When and how to use each
  • Lesson Frameworks: The ESA model (Engage, Study, Activate), PPP model
  • Task-Based Language Teaching (TBLT) in the online context
  • Utilizing Digital Tools: Zoom, Google Docs, Kahoot, etc.
  • Virtual Classroom Management: Strategies for engagement

Module 3: Designing Curriculum and Materials for Online Adult Learners

Introduction: Learn how to create relevant and effective course content for adult learners in an online setting. This module covers needs analysis, material selection, and adaptation for diverse contexts.

  • Conducting a Needs Analysis: Tailoring content to learner goals
  • Sourcing and Adapting Authentic Materials
  • Content Creation Strategies: Making lessons learner-centered and culturally sensitive
  • Addressing Connectivity Challenges: Strategies for low-bandwidth environments
  • Creating Engaging Multimedia Content

Module 4: Assessment, Feedback, and Learner Autonomy in Online Teaching

Introduction: This module covers the critical aspects of assessing learner progress, providing effective feedback, and fostering learner autonomy. You'll discover how to empower your students to take ownership of their learning journey.

  • Formative and Summative Assessment in Online Environments
  • Best Practices for Giving Constructive and Timely Feedback
  • Strategies for Promoting Learner Autonomy and Self-Evaluation
  • Online Assessment Tools and Techniques (e.g., audio/video submissions)
